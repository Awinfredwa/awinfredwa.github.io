Why It Mattered

The work turned a manual power-user feature into an assisted workflow that was safer to try, easier to roll back, and practical enough to become part of a subscription experience.

AI-Assisted CSS Customization at Real Scale

JanitorAI users were already customizing their profiles with CSS, but the workflow was mostly manual and easiest for power users. I helped turn that into a product experience: a React and TypeScript customization surface with a Monaco-style CSS editor, live preview sandbox, edit history, and an AI editing flow that could generate or revise profile styles from natural-language requests.

The hard part was not just generating CSS. Users needed room to experiment creatively without breaking their profile, losing previous work, or publishing unsafe output. I worked on the surrounding product and operational pieces that made the feature reliable: preview before publish, rollback through edit history, guardrails around AI-generated CSS, and controlled rollout through CI/CD and Statsig experiments.

Product Surface

The editor flow gave users a place to write CSS directly, preview changes in a sandboxed profile environment, save versions, and publish only after checking the result. That kept the power-user workflow intact while making customization less intimidating for everyone else.

AI Editing Flow

The AI workflow helped users describe what they wanted in plain language, then generated or modified CSS against their existing profile style. The goal was to make the assistant feel like a styling collaborator, not a black box that overwrote the user's work.

Reliability and Rollout

Because this ran on a large consumer product, reliability mattered as much as creativity. The system needed to be cheap enough to operate, safe enough to expose to real users, and measurable enough to ship through experiments instead of a one-shot launch.

What I Took Away

This is my strongest example of building full-stack operational tooling around a messy real user workflow: take something flexible but fragile, wrap it in useful product controls, and make it dependable enough for daily use at scale.
